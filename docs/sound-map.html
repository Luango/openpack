OpenPack — Pack-Opening Sound Map

A senior sound-design review & polish pass over the pack-opening experience. Every cue is synthesized in Web Audio — no audio files. Targeted to a premium Pokémon-TCG-Pocket-grade open, tuned for a phone speaker.

The engine was already well-built — a real master bus, a sustained speed-tracked tear, a tier-escalating chime. Two things held it back: gain-staging that fought itself on a phone (a −10 dB limiter crushing everything, plus an inaudible 58 Hz sub pumping the mix) and a payoff curve that visually escalated but audibly settled (the riser dissolved into dead air; rarity never reached the tear or the open). Fixed both, then closed the silent gaps and the real bugs. 23 cues, 6 of them new.

Engine & mix — re-tuned for a phone speaker

Loudness that actually lands

  • Limiter raised −10 → −3 dB so it catches stacked peaks instead of crushing every hit, plus a post-limiter makeup gain. The mix is loud again.
  • Open-burst sub re-aimed 58→34 Hz → ~90→55 Hz — felt on a phone instead of inaudibly pumping the limiter.
  • A 24 dB/oct high-pass at 45 Hz on the bus scrubs rumble a phone can't reproduce.

A reverb that rings, not washes

  • Reverb return band-limited (~240 Hz–10 kHz) — the tail is air, not mud.
  • Chime sends made drier and tier-ramped, so a rare-pull chime is a distinct ringing hit on a small speaker.
  • Struck-bell envelopes — octave sparkles decay faster than the fundamentals, so it rings like glass.

Rarity reaches the big moments

  • The two longest/loudest moments — the tear and the open burst — now escalate with the rarest card inside (tellTier).
  • revealImpact scales from a gentle Double-Rare thump to a sub-reinforced Hyper takeover — a monotonic crescendo.

Accessibility & robustness

A real volume / mute control

  • Top-right mute toggle + volume slider, persisted (shared with the gallery). Collapses to a mute button on a phone so it clears the nav.
  • Audio is now decoupled from prefers-reduced-motion — a motion preference no longer silences the jackpot. The volume control owns quieting.

Bugs fixed

  • Split the shared hover/scratch throttle — hovering the grid no longer starves the foil scratch mid-drag.
  • Per-cue pitch/level variation on every repeated cue (flick, set-down, spark, hover, sparkle) — no more machine-gun.
  • Capture-phase unlock + tab-hide suspend() for a clean cold-start and battery.
Implemented in src/sfx.js (engine + all cues), wired through src/pack.js & src/reveal.js, with the control + CSS in pack.html. Verified in-browser: the full lifecycle (tear → open → rare-hit reveal → end-of-pack → reseal) runs with zero console errors, all 23 cues build their audio graphs cleanly, and the control persists. Note: sound can't be judged by ear in a headless preview — the final mix calls are yours to confirm on a device.
