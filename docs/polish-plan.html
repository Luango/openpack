Polish the rip to its maximum

Where it stands: already a high-polish prototype — two-stage paper tear, contact-synced landing, a monotonic per-tier HIT table, synthesized audio with a reverb bus, and serious mobile perf work. So "max polish" = closing the felt extremes and removing the demo-credibility blockers — not rebuilding. Every new effect stays transform/opacity-only and reduced-motion-safe (see guardrails).

TIER 1

The felt extremes — the heart of it

Weight of the rip, size of the hit, length of the suspense, payoff of the haul. This is where “polish” is won.

  • Heavier tear

    Pre-tear foil resistance (the pack bulges a touch under the finger before it gives); crack width keys off pull speed; a commit pop (scale punch + micro screen-shake); a distance-quantized haptic ratchet every ~20px + a crackle accent — so the rip answers the hand, not the clock.

    HighM

  • The HIT punches the card, not just the backdrop

    Today card-enter is identical for a Common and a Hyper. Make the entrance tier-scaled: gentle set-down for a common → hard contact-dip + shake for a chase, layered on the existing rays/flash/stamp.

    HighM

  • Land the riser on a downbeat

    The anticipation riser climbs then dies into silence. Fire a tier-scaled impact boom (reuse the burst voice) synced to the uncover frame + a brief mix duck — the suspense finally lands.

    HighM

  • Longer, deeper suspense

    Pulse the colour leak behind the next card, dim the world further, and hold the top-tier beat a little longer before the flash. Add a faint shockwave so a Double Rare isn't a flat shimmer.

    MedM

  • Haul-summary payoff

    “That's the pack!” throws away the collection dopamine. Fan all 5 cards out, the rarest glowing, with a tiny rarity tally — then “Open another”. This is what rewards the loop.

    HighL

  • Weight on every advance

    A flicked common flings in the drag direction with a deck recoil + a soft contact sound + a small haptic — not a silent straight-up slide.

    HighM
TIER 2

Depth & atmosphere

The layer that turns “nice” into “alive”. Magnetism before the tear, air around the sound.

  • Quiet reactive ambient bed

    A sub-perceptual drone on unlock that lifts with idle-heat and ducks under hits. Silence→tear contrast is what makes the rip punch.

    HighM

  • Reactive sealed pack

    Idle pointer / device-tilt drives the foil sheen + a slight parallax — the pack feels held, not photographed. Pauses cleanly on .tearing.

    HighL

  • First-run gesture hint

    A one-shot ghost stroke tracing the tear along the crimp after the pack arms, so a first-timer sees the drag. Self-dismisses on first touch.

    HighM

  • Living audio + foreshadow

    Humanize repeated cues (detune/gain jitter so the chime never machine-guns); raise the rarity-tell heat floor + scale spark intensity by heat so a chase whispers before it shouts.

    MedM

  • Matte paper flecks

    The foil debris currently glows like sparks. Mix in the (already-built but unused) matte chip sprite ~3:1 so torn paper reads as paper.

    MedS
TIER 3

Mobile delivery & perf hardening

The brief is mobile-first. Pay down the last weights so it loads fast and never drops a frame.

  • Shrink the hero asset

    The 2.7MB pack.png is the LCP and the wrong codec. Downscale to display size as WebP/AVIF + fetchpriority=high. Biggest mobile load win.

    HighM

  • Pre-warm audio off the hot path

    Build the reverb impulse in requestIdleCallback (shorter, mono) so the first tear doesn't hitch while it synthesizes.

    MedM

  • Trim idle cost

    Drop will-change on the idle pack + the six float-cards at rest; throttle tearMove sfx to ~60Hz and reuse a cached point.

    MedS

⚙ Guardrails — don't regress the careful work

  • New VFX = transform/opacity only. No animated filter (the documented mobile slice-lag).
  • prefers-reduced-motion honored on every new cue (shake, pop, ambient, gesture hint).
  • Reactive idle specular must pause on .tearing; no new idle will-change.
  • Exit distances stay in pixels (iOS won't animate vmax/vh targets).
  • HIT escalation stays monotonic — a tier never out-punches the one above it.
  • Mind audio headroom (limiter −10 / ratio 12); duck under the chime so layers don't clip.
